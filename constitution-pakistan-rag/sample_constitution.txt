PART I - INTRODUCTORY

Article 1: Pakistan shall be a Federal Republic to be known as the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.

Article 2: Islam shall be the State religion of Pakistan. The State shall ensure the elimination of all forms of exploitation and the gradual fulfillment of the fundamental principle.

Article 3: The State shall ensure the elimination of all forms of exploitation and the gradual fulfillment of the fundamental principle, from each according to his ability, to each according to his work.

Article 4: To enjoy the protection of law and to be treated in accordance with law is the inalienable right of every citizen.

Article 5: Loyalty to the State is the basic duty of every citizen.

Article 6: Any person who abrogates or attempts or conspires to abrogate, subverts or attempts or conspires to subvert the Constitution by use of force or show of force or by other unconstitutional means shall be guilty of high treason.

Article 7: The State shall ensure the elimination of all forms of exploitation and the gradual fulfillment of the fundamental principle.

Article 8: Laws inconsistent with or in derogation of fundamental rights to be void.

PART II - FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS AND PRINCIPLES OF POLICY

Article 9: Security of person - No person shall be deprived of life or liberty save in accordance with law.

Article 10: Safeguards as to arrest and detention - Every person who is arrested and detained in custody shall be produced before a magistrate within a period of twenty-four hours.

Article 11: Slavery, forced labour, etc., prohibited - Slavery is non-existent and forbidden and no law shall permit or facilitate its introduction into Pakistan in any form.

Article 12: Protection against retrospective punishment - No law shall authorize the punishment of a person for an act or omission that was not punishable by law at the time of the act or omission.

Article 13: Protection against double punishment and self-incrimination - No person shall be prosecuted or punished for the same offence more than once.

Article 14: Inviolability of dignity of man, etc. - The dignity of man and, subject to law, the privacy of home, shall be inviolable.

Article 15: Freedom of movement, etc. - Every citizen shall have the right to remain in, and, subject to any reasonable restriction imposed by law in the public interest, enter and move freely throughout Pakistan.

Article 16: Freedom of assembly - Every citizen shall have the right to assemble peacefully and without arms, subject to any reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the interest of public order.

Article 17: Freedom of association - Every citizen shall have the right to form associations or unions, subject to any reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the interest of sovereignty or integrity of Pakistan, public order or morality.

Article 18: Freedom of trade, business or profession - Subject to such qualifications, if any, as may be prescribed by law, every citizen shall have the right to enter upon any lawful profession or occupation, and to conduct any lawful trade or business.

Article 19: Freedom of speech, etc. - Every citizen shall have the right to freedom of speech and expression, and there shall be freedom of the press, subject to any reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the interest of the glory of Islam or the integrity, security or defence of Pakistan or any part thereof.

Article 20: Freedom to profess religion and to manage religious institutions - Subject to law, public order and morality, every citizen shall have the right to profess, practice and propagate his religion.

Article 21: Safeguard against taxation for purposes of any particular religion - No person shall be compelled to pay any special tax the proceeds of which are to be spent on the propagation or maintenance of any religion other than his own.

Article 22: Safeguards as to educational institutions in respect of religion, etc. - No person attending any educational institution shall be required to receive religious instruction, or take part in any religious ceremony, or attend religious worship.

Article 23: Provision as to property - Every citizen shall have the right to acquire, hold and dispose of property in any part of Pakistan, subject to the Constitution and any reasonable restrictions imposed by law in the public interest.

Article 24: Protection of property rights - No person shall be compulsorily deprived of his property save in accordance with law.

Article 25: Equality of citizens - All citizens are equal before law and are entitled to equal protection of law.

Article 26: Non-discrimination in respect of access to public places - In respect of access to places of public entertainment or resort, not intended for religious purposes only, there shall be no discrimination against any citizen on the ground only of race, religion, caste, sex, residence or place of birth.

Article 27: Safeguard against discrimination in services - No citizen otherwise qualified for appointment in the service of Pakistan shall be discriminated against in respect of any such appointment on the ground only of race, religion, caste, sex, residence or place of birth.

Article 28: Preservation of language, script and culture - Subject to Article 251, any section of citizens having a distinct language, script or culture shall have the right to preserve and promote the same and subject to law, establish institutions for that purpose.

PART III - THE FEDERATION OF PAKISTAN

Article 29: The President - There shall be a President of Pakistan who shall be the Head of State and shall represent the unity of the Republic.

Article 30: The Prime Minister - There shall be a Prime Minister of Pakistan who shall be the Head of Government and shall be elected by the National Assembly.

Article 31: The Parliament - There shall be a Parliament of Pakistan consisting of the President and two Houses to be known respectively as the National Assembly and the Senate.

Article 32: The National Assembly - The National Assembly shall consist of three hundred and forty-two members to be elected by direct and free vote in accordance with law.

Article 33: The Senate - The Senate shall consist of one hundred and four members, of whom fourteen shall be elected by each Provincial Assembly.

Article 34: The Supreme Court - There shall be a Supreme Court of Pakistan, a High Court for each Province and such other courts as may be established by law.

Article 35: The High Courts - Each High Court shall consist of a Chief Justice and so many other Judges as may be determined by law or, until so determined, as may be fixed by the President.

Article 36: The Federal Shariat Court - There shall be a Federal Shariat Court consisting of not more than eight Muslim Judges, including the Chief Justice.

Article 37: The Election Commission - There shall be an Election Commission of Pakistan consisting of a Chief Election Commissioner and such number of Election Commissioners as may be determined by law.

Article 38: The Auditor General - There shall be an Auditor General of Pakistan who shall be appointed by the President.

Article 39: The Attorney General - The President shall appoint a person, being a person qualified to be appointed a Judge of the Supreme Court, to be Attorney General for Pakistan.

Article 40: The Federal Public Service Commission - There shall be a Federal Public Service Commission whose Chairman and members shall be appointed by the President.

Article 41: The Provincial Public Service Commissions - There shall be a Provincial Public Service Commission for each Province whose Chairman and members shall be appointed by the Governor of the Province.

Article 42: The Federal Ombudsman - There shall be a Federal Ombudsman to be known as Wafaqi Mohtasib who shall be appointed by the President.

Article 43: The Provincial Ombudsmen - There shall be a Provincial Ombudsman for each Province to be known as the Mohtasib-e-Suba who shall be appointed by the Governor of the Province.

Article 44: The Federal Investigation Agency - There shall be a Federal Investigation Agency whose Director General shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 45: The National Accountability Bureau - There shall be a National Accountability Bureau whose Chairman shall be appointed by the President.

Article 46: The Federal Board of Revenue - There shall be a Federal Board of Revenue whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 47: The State Bank of Pakistan - There shall be a State Bank of Pakistan whose Governor shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 48: The Securities and Exchange Commission - There shall be a Securities and Exchange Commission whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 49: The Competition Commission - There shall be a Competition Commission whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 50: The National Electric Power Regulatory Authority - There shall be a National Electric Power Regulatory Authority whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 51: The Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority - There shall be an Oil and Gas Regulatory Authority whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 52: The Pakistan Telecommunication Authority - There shall be a Pakistan Telecommunication Authority whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 53: The Frequency Allocation Board - There shall be a Frequency Allocation Board whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 54: The Pakistan Electronic Media Regulatory Authority - There shall be a Pakistan Electronic Media Regulatory Authority whose Chairman shall be appointed by the Federal Government.

Article 55: The Council of Common Interests - There shall be a Council of Common Interests, in this Chapter referred to as the Council, to be constituted by the President.

Article 56: The National Economic Council - There shall be a National Economic Council consisting of the Prime Minister, who shall be its Chairman, and such other members as the President may determine.

Article 57: The National Finance Commission - The President shall constitute a National Finance Commission consisting of the Federal Minister of Finance, the Provincial Ministers of Finance, and such other persons as may be appointed by the President after consultation with the Governors of the Provinces.

Article 58: The Islamic Ideology Council - There shall be an Islamic Ideology Council to be known as the Islamic Ideology Council of Pakistan.

Article 59: The Council of Islamic Ideology - The Council shall consist of such number of members as the President may determine, but the number shall not be less than eight or more than twenty.

Article 60: The Federal Shariat Court - There shall be a Federal Shariat Court consisting of not more than eight Muslim Judges, including the Chief Justice.

Article 61: The Supreme Judicial Council - There shall be a Supreme Judicial Council of Pakistan.

Article 62: The Judicial Commission - There shall be a Judicial Commission of Pakistan.

Article 63: The National Judicial Policy Making Committee - There shall be a National Judicial Policy Making Committee.

Article 64: The Law and Justice Commission - There shall be a Law and Justice Commission of Pakistan.

Article 65: The Federal Judicial Academy - There shall be a Federal Judicial Academy.

Article 66: The Provincial Judicial Academies - There shall be a Provincial Judicial Academy for each Province.

Article 67: The National Judicial Academy - There shall be a National Judicial Academy.

Article 68: The Federal Judicial Academy - There shall be a Federal Judicial Academy.

Article 69: The Provincial Judicial Academies - There shall be a Provincial Judicial Academy for each Province.

Article 70: The National Judicial Academy - There shall be a National Judicial Academy.
